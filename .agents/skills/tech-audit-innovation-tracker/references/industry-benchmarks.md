# Industry Benchmarks — Maintenance vs Innovation Ratio

Use these benchmarks to contextualize the user's results. These are approximate
ranges compiled from Gartner, McKinsey, and Deloitte research through 2024.

## Overall IT Industry Average
- **Maintenance: 60-70%** | **Innovation: 30-40%**
- The "keep-the-lights-on trap" threshold is generally at >75% maintenance

## By Industry

| Industry | Maintenance % | Innovation % | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Financial Services | 65-75 | 25-35 | Heavy regulatory/compliance burden |
| Healthcare | 60-70 | 30-40 | EHR maintenance, HIPAA compliance |
| Retail / E-commerce | 50-60 | 40-50 | Competitive pressure drives innovation |
| Technology / SaaS | 40-55 | 45-60 | Product-led, innovation is the business |
| Manufacturing | 65-75 | 25-35 | Legacy ERP/MES systems |
| Government / Public Sector | 75-85 | 15-25 | Budget constraints, legacy systems |
| Telecommunications | 60-70 | 30-40 | Network maintenance + digital transformation |
| Energy / Utilities | 70-80 | 20-30 | Critical infrastructure, regulatory |
| Education | 65-75 | 25-35 | Budget constraints |
| Media / Entertainment | 45-55 | 45-55 | Content platform innovation |

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Innovation % | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Leader** | >50% | Innovation is the primary focus; maintenance is automated/optimized |
| **Advanced** | 40-50% | Healthy balance; active modernization programs |
| **Average** | 30-40% | Industry norm; some innovation but constrained |
| **Below Average** | 20-30% | Maintenance-heavy; struggling to innovate |
| **Critical** | <20% | Trapped in maintenance; existential risk |

## Key Insights for Recommendations

1. **The 70/30 threshold**: Organizations spending >70% on maintenance typically can't
   self-correct without executive intervention and a deliberate program.

2. **Quick wins for shifting ratio**:
   - Automate manual operational tasks (monitoring, deployments, testing)
   - Consolidate redundant systems
   - Retire unused applications (most orgs have 20-30% zombie apps)
   - Move commodity workloads to SaaS (email, CRM, ITSM)
   - Outsource commodity maintenance

3. **Common misconceptions**:
   - "We just need more budget" — usually the ratio stays the same with more money
   - "Cloud migration will fix it" — only if accompanied by process change
   - "Technical debt paydown is maintenance" — it can be innovation if strategic

4. **Trend matters more than snapshot**: An organization at 35% innovation but trending
   up from 20% is healthier than one at 40% trending down from 55%.
